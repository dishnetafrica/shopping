Welcome to Family Shopper

This panel is your control centre. Customers order over WhatsApp (the bot takes the order automatically) or you create orders here on POS. You then confirm, dispatch with a rider, and the customer gets a live tracking link. Every order has a number like FS-1024.

1. Signing in

1
Enter your WhatsApp number (with country code) and tap Send code.
2
You get a code on WhatsApp — type it in to log in. You stay logged in on this device.
3
To sign out, use Logout (top-right or bottom of the sidebar).

2. Dashboard

The first screen shows live counts: total orders, pending, out for delivery, delivered, cancelled, today's orders, products and customers. It refreshes on its own every 20 seconds.

3. Orders

The Orders page lists every order with its FS number, date, customer, status and total. WhatsApp orders appear here automatically within seconds.

  • Search by FS number, customer name or phone.
  • Tap 👁 to open the invoice.

Inside an order (invoice)

  • Change status from the dropdown (New → Confirmed → Packed → Out for delivery → Delivered).
  • Add / remove items and edit quantities, then Save.
  • Print / PDF the invoice, Cancel the order, or Apply credit if the customer has store credit.
  • Dispatch — assign a rider (see section 5).
Tip: the customer's WhatsApp number is captured automatically — you don't need to ask for it.

5. Dispatch (assign a rider)

1
Open the order and find the Dispatch section (or use the Dispatch page).
2
Pick the rider and confirm their phone, then tap Dispatch.
3
The customer instantly gets a WhatsApp with a live tracking link showing status, the rider, items and a map. The order status becomes Out for delivery.

6. POS — walk-in / phone order

1
Open POS. Add products by scanning a barcode (see section 13), by typing in the search box, or by tapping a product card. Set quantities.
2
Add the customer name/phone, any charges or discount.
3
Tap checkout — it creates an order with its own FS number, just like a WhatsApp order.

7. Returns & store credit

Open an order and choose Returns. Pick how to resolve it:

  • Adjust total — lower the order total for returned items.
  • Store credit — give the customer credit for next time.
  • Refund — mark a refund as pending.
  • Redeem credit — apply existing credit to this order.

The customer gets a WhatsApp confirming it, and any store credit is tracked by their phone number and shown on their future orders.

8. Products & price list

The catalogue (names, prices, stock) lives in your Google Sheet price list. Edit prices and stock there and they flow through to both the panel and the WhatsApp bot.

  • From the Products page you can edit a product and add a photo (URL or upload).
  • Scan to edit: scan a barcode on the Products page to jump straight to that product's price/stock (see section 13).
  • The Category page groups products for quick browsing.
Prices the bot quotes always come from this price list — keep it accurate. If a customer asks for something missing, add it (or add a keyword) so the bot finds it next time.

9. Riders

On the Riders page, add / edit / remove riders (name + phone). These are the riders you pick when dispatching.

10. Customers

The Customers page shows each customer's order history, total spend and average — handy before calling them back.

11. Settings

  • Store profile — name, phone, address, email (these print on every invoice).
  • Delivery pricing — base fee, per-km rate, minimum, free-over threshold, and your store location (the delivery fee is calculated from the customer's shared map pin).
  • Currency & exchange rates — set 1 USD = ? UGX and 1 USD = ? SSP. Update these when the street rate moves (see section 14).
  • Branches & inventory — add branches and choose shared-pool or per-branch stock.

12. How the WhatsApp bot works

Most customers are Gujarati- and Hindi-speaking, and the bot talks to them in their own language:

  • It greets Gujarati customers with Jai Shree Krishna 🙏 (and mirrors however a customer greets — Namaste, Salaam, Hello).
  • It understands mixed Gujarati/Hindi/English — e.g. khand joiye che, cheeni chahiye, tel nu bhav — and finds the right product and price.
  • It takes the order, asks quantity and location in the customer's language, and confirms.
  • It quotes prices in the customer's currency automatically — a +211 (South Sudan) number sees SSP, a +256 (Uganda) number sees UGX, and anyone who asks "in USD" gets USD (see section 14).
  • You and the team get a staff alert on WhatsApp, and the order appears here with its FS number. The customer also receives their order number, and a tracking link once you dispatch.

13. Barcode scanning

You can add or look up products by scanning instead of searching — on POS, on the order invoice (add item), and on the Products page (jump to edit). Two ways to scan:

  • USB / Bluetooth scanner — the supermarket kind. It works like a keyboard: it types the barcode into the "⌨ Scan" box and the item is added. No setup. Best for a fixed checkout.
  • 📷 Scan button — uses the phone or laptop camera (works on Chrome / Android). If a device has no camera scanner, use a USB scanner or type the code.

About 72% of products carry a real barcode (EAN/UPC). The rest — loose plastics, repacks, produce — use the internal item code instead; print your own shelf label for those and they'll scan too. If a scan finds nothing, the panel says "No product for [code]" rather than adding the wrong item.

Prices stay in your Google Sheet price list in UGX. Scanning only looks products up — it never changes a price.

14. Prices in UGX / SSP / USD

The shop runs across Uganda and South Sudan, so prices can show in three currencies. UGX is always the base — the price list, the orders saved, your reports and the staff alerts all stay in UGX. Only what's shown is converted.

  • In the panel, pick the currency from the dropdown in the top bar (next to the branch picker). It opens in SSP on a South Sudan device and UGX on a Ugandan one, and you can switch to USD anytime — handy when a customer asks in dollars.
  • The bot quotes each customer automatically: +211 → SSP, +256 → UGX, "in USD" → USD.
  • Rates live in Settings → Currency & exchange rates (e.g. 1 USD = 3,750 UGX = 7,000 SSP). SSP prices are rounded to a clean figure (nearest 100) so you never quote an odd number.
When you edit a product's price, type it in UGX (the base). The SSP/USD figures are worked out from it — editing the SSP figure directly is not possible, on purpose, so the price list can't drift.

15. Quick tips

  • Search orders by FS-#### when a customer references their number.
  • Always Dispatch with a rider so the customer gets their tracking link automatically.
  • Keep the price list and stock up to date — it drives both the panel and the bot.

Technical overview (for admin)

This is a condensed reference. The full handoff (schema, SQL, credentials) is in PLATFORM_HANDOFF.md.

Architecture

  • WhatsApp bot — an n8n workflow (Evolution API + OpenAI gpt-5.4-mini + Postgres memory). Matches messages to the catalogue, takes orders, alerts staff, writes orders to Postgres.
  • Seller panel — this app; a thin client that calls n8n webhooks and polls every 20s.
  • Backend — n8n webhooks + data stores.
  • Currency — display layer only. Base is UGX everywhere (price list, Postgres, staff alerts). Panel converts in ugx()/convFromUgx(); the bot converts in the Extract Order node (visible text only). Rates: 1 USD = 3,750 UGX = 7,000 SSP; SSP rounded to nearest 100.
  • Barcodes — panel-side lookup. Recovered from the price-list Keywords (the Barcode_1/2/3 columns are corrupted to scientific notation — do not use), with Item_Code as fallback. Scan = USB keyboard-wedge or the camera's BarcodeDetector.

Conventions

  • Order id = Postgres id (panel sends it as the row param). FS-#### = order_no.
  • Track link: /webhook/track?o=<id>&t=<token>; dispatch sets the token.
  • The bot workflow is generated by build scripts (build_full.py + build2.py) — don't hand-edit the exported JSON.
  • Bot currency: Extract Order node, top of code — FORCE_CUR ('auto'/'UGX'/'SSP'/'USD'), SSP_ROUND, and USD_UGX/USD_SSP rates. Keep rates in sync with the panel until a shared store exists.
  • Login allow-list is server-side in the dashboard-auth-request workflow — never in the panel.
  • Ensure Redis has persistence (returns/store-credit and the order counter live there).
