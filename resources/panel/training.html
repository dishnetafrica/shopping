MES Staff Training — find your role, learn your screen

Each person below has one or two screens to learn. Do your part well and the whole factory gets faster — that's the point of the system.

Why this system exists: to raise machine efficiency (OEE = Availability × Performance × Quality). Every order is entered once and reused everywhere; the system plans the work, records what actually happened, and alerts the right person the moment time is being lost. SAP stays the book of record — this plans, times, records and alerts.
Log in once at /app with your WhatsApp number (a one-time code arrives on WhatsApp — no password). Then open your screen below.

Production Operator & Supervisor

extrusion · printing · cutting · blending/panel/production

You record what actually happens on your machine. This is the most important data in the whole system — the efficiency numbers, the alerts, the daily report all come from what you enter here. Use a phone or tablet at the machine.

Recording a run

  1. Open /panel/production on your phone.
  2. Tap your machine in the top row.
  3. Tap the job you are running (it shows the indent, product and target kg/hr).
  4. When you start, tap Now next to Start time. When you finish, tap Now next to End time.
  5. Enter produced pcs and produced kg, and scrap kg if any.
  6. If the machine stopped, choose the stop reason (Breakdown / Power / Material).
  7. Set QC — Pass or Reject — for the run.
  8. Tap Save entry. You'll see the run's hours, output and OEE.

Do

  • Log as it happens — start when you start, end when you stop.
  • Enter the real produced and scrap figures, even on a bad run.
  • Always pick a stop reason when the machine stops.

Don't

  • Don't fill it all in at end of shift from memory — the numbers drift.
  • Don't hide scrap or a reject — that's exactly what we need to fix.
  • Don't guess kg — weigh it.

why it matters If a stop or reject isn't logged, the system can't alert anyone and the loss stays invisible. Honest, prompt entries are how the factory finds and fixes the biggest time-wasters.

Production Manager (PD.M)

machine allocation · the daily efficiency review/panel/planning · /panel/dashboard

You decide which machine runs which job and when. The system then works out how long it will take and when it will finish — and the operators see the jobs you create.

Planning a job

  1. Open /panel/planning. Each open indent is a card, with its processes listed.
  2. For each process, pick the machine, enter the running speed and the planned start.
  3. Enter the output (kg/hr) you expect — the system uses your figure as the source of truth.
  4. Tap Plan. The system fills in required hours and the planned end, and marks the indent Planned.
  5. Repeat for the other processes. The jobs now appear on the operators' screen.

The daily review (5 minutes)

  1. Open /panel/dashboard.
  2. Look at Machine OEE — worst first. The machine at the top is losing the most.
  3. Look at Downtime by reason. Attack the top bar — that's your biggest recurring loss.
  4. Check the Machine board — any machine idle (low utilisation) or overbooked?
  5. Pick one thing to fix today. Re-check tomorrow.

tip Don't try to fix everything. The system's job is to make the single biggest loss impossible to ignore — go after that one.

Sales — SDH & Sales Coordinator

create the order indent/panel/indents

You turn a confirmed order into an indent — the controlled document that tells the factory exactly what to make.

Creating an indent

  1. Open /panel/indents → New.
  2. Pick the customer and item; the product name fills in. Enter order qty, mixing qty and the required date.
  3. Tick the processes the job needs (Blending / Extrusion / Printing / Cutting). Only the relevant spec sections appear.
  4. Fill the specs for each process (width, gauge, colours, bag size, etc.).
  5. Add the blending materials with their % per extruder — the kg are worked out for you. Watch for the ✓ (balanced) or ⚠ (percentages don't add to 100).
  6. Tap Save indent — a controlled indent number is assigned (e.g. 003-090626).

Repeat order? Clone it.

  1. Pick the previous indent from the open indent list at the top.
  2. Tap Clone ("same as last order"). All specs and the recipe copy across.
  3. Change only what's different (qty, required date), then Save — a new number is assigned.

Do

  • Always set a required date — it drives the delay-risk alert.
  • Make the blend percentages add to 100% per extruder (✓).

Don't

  • Don't leave the product/specs vague — the floor builds exactly what you write.
  • Don't retype a repeat order — clone it.

Accounts Clerk & Admin

keep the masters correct/app → "Win World" group

The masters are the foundation. If an item's dimensions or a machine's details are wrong, every calculation built on them is wrong. You manage these in the business panel at /app, under the Win World menu group.

What you maintain

  1. Items — code, name, width, length, gauge. The gram/pcs is calculated for you (W × L × gauge ÷ 3300); just keep the dimensions right.
  2. Machines — the 22 machines with process and max speed. Mark a machine inactive when it's out of service so it can't be planned.
  3. Materials — the resins, masterbatch and additives used in blending.
  4. Customers — name, credit-limit days, and the ageing / overdue days (kept in step with SAP). Anything over 30 days overdue shows red — that needs MD approval before the order proceeds.

remember SAP is still the book of record for items, prices and ageing. These masters are the planning copy — keep them in step with SAP.

Quality Control

Supervisor · QC · Section-Head QC/panel/production

Quality is one of the three parts of OEE. Every run gets a QC verdict, and a reject is flagged immediately so it can be fixed before more material is wasted.

On each run

  1. On the production entry for the run, set QC to Pass or Reject.
  2. A Reject sends an instant WhatsApp alert to production so the issue is caught early.
  3. Record the scrap kg so the quality figure on the dashboard is accurate.
⚠
QC REJECT alerts go to the production team the moment you mark a run rejected — the faster the catch, the less material wasted.

Stores & Maintenance

act on the alertsWhatsApp

You don't enter data — you respond. When a machine stops, the system messages you on WhatsApp within seconds so the machine isn't sitting idle waiting for someone to notice.

⛔
Material Shortage stop → Stores. Get the material to the machine fast.
⛔
Machine Breakdown stop → Maintenance. Respond and clear it.

to receive alerts Your WhatsApp number must be added (by the admin) to the recipient list with your role — stores or maintenance. Every minute saved here is a minute the machine is running instead of waiting.

MD & Auditor

read the efficiency picture/panel/dashboard

One screen tells you how the factory is performing and where it's losing time.

What to read

  1. Avg OEE and produced-vs-ordered kg — the headline health.
  2. First-pass yield — how much came out good first time.
  3. Machine OEE, worst first — where the losses are concentrated.
  4. Downtime by reason — what keeps stopping the line.
  5. Use the 7d / 30d / All toggle to see the trend.
📦
You and Sales also receive a DELAY RISK alert when a job's planned finish is past the customer's required date — early enough to do something about it.

use it in the daily meeting Ask one question: "What is the worst machine / biggest downtime reason, and what are we doing about it?"
